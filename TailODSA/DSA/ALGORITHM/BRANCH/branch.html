BRANCH AND BOUND ALGORITHM

What is a Branch and Bound Algorithm?

Branch and Bound is an algorithm design paradigm for solving combinatorial and optimization problems. It systematically enumerates candidate solutions by means of a tree structure.

Applications

Branch and Bound is used in various applications such as the traveling salesman problem, job scheduling, and integer programming.

How it Works

The algorithm divides the problem into smaller subproblems (branching) and calculates bounds on the best possible solution within those subproblems to prune the search space.

Advantages & Disadvantages

Advantages: Can find optimal solutions and is flexible in handling different types of problems.
Disadvantages: Can be slow for large problems due to the exhaustive search nature.

Time and Space Complexity

Time Complexity: Highly variable depending on the problem structure.
Space Complexity: O(b^d), where b is the branching factor and d is the depth of the search tree.
